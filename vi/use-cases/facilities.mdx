Facilities management runs on trust you can't always verify: a contractor who says they were on-site for four hours, a maintenance company whose technician completed the quarterly HVAC service, a cleaning crew whose shift supervisor signed off on a building that nobody from your team inspected. When that trust is misplaced, SLAs are missed, assets degrade, and the costs show up later — in emergency repairs, regulatory penalties, or dissatisfied tenants. Supa gives facilities managers GPS-verified proof of contractor presence, structured maintenance records, and live visibility across every building without requiring a site visit for every job.

## The challenge

Multi-site facilities operations face three compounding problems. First, verifying that contractors and cleaning crews are actually on-site when they say they are — without stationing your own staff to watch them. Second, tracking maintenance SLAs across dozens of buildings and hundreds of recurring tasks without things falling through the cracks. Third, coordinating incident reporting and corrective actions across a team that's spread across multiple sites and working different shifts.

The conventional answer is more phone calls, more visits, and more spreadsheets. These don't scale. They also don't produce the audit trail you need when a contractor disputes a missed SLA or when a regulator asks for your maintenance records.

## How Supa solves it

Supa enforces on-site presence through GPS-locked checklists: a contractor or staff member can only submit a completed checklist from within a configured geofence around the building. Every submission carries a timestamp and GPS coordinate. If someone attempts to submit from off-site, the checklist is blocked.

For recurring maintenance, Supa schedules checklists automatically and escalates overdue completions. When a task is completed, photo proof is required before the task can be closed. For incidents, any staff member can raise a report from their phone — with photos, location, and priority — and the investigation workflow ensures nothing is left unresolved.

## How GPS verification prevents false reporting

<Steps>
  <Step title="Configure the geofence for each building">
    Set a geofence radius around each building or site in Supa. You control the boundary — from a tight perimeter to a wider zone that accommodates car parks and outbuildings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign checklists to contractors and staff">
    Contractors and cleaning crews receive their scheduled checklists on their phone. They can see the tasks, but cannot submit until they're within the geofence.
  </Step>
  <Step title="GPS and timestamp captured automatically">
    When a checklist is submitted, Supa records the GPS coordinates and timestamp. This data is visible to building managers and stored in the audit log.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Off-site attempts are blocked and flagged">
    If a contractor tries to submit from outside the geofence, the submission is rejected. The attempt is logged, and a manager alert can be configured for repeated violations.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
GPS verification requires that contractors and staff complete checklists using the Supa mobile app with location permissions enabled. Instruct contractors to enable location access before their first assignment.
</Warning>
