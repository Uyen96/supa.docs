Heads Up is Supa's broadcast tool for one-way communication to your frontline workforce. When you need everyone in the organisation — or a specific group — to receive and acknowledge a message, Heads Up gives you the delivery confirmation that an email or group chat cannot: a real-time list of who has read it and who has not.

## Send a Heads Up

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Heads Up">
    In Supa, navigate to **Heads Up** in the sidebar and click **New Announcement**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compose your message">
    Write the announcement title and body text. Keep the message clear and direct — frontline staff may read it between tasks on a mobile screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add media">
    Click **Attach** to add an image, video, or file. Supported formats include JPEG, PNG, MP4, and PDF.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your audience">
    Under **Send to**, choose one or more of the following:
    - **Entire organisation** — all active users
    - **Location** — all staff at one or more specific sites
    - **Role** — all users with a specific role (e.g., all Shift Supervisors)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set priority">
    Toggle **High priority** for urgent safety alerts. High-priority messages trigger an immediate push notification and appear at the top of the recipient's Heads Up list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send and monitor">
    Click **Send**. The announcement is delivered immediately. In the **Heads Up** dashboard, watch the read receipt count update in real time as recipients open the message.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Use cases

- **New procedure rollout** — Announce a change to a cleaning or preparation procedure and require staff to acknowledge they have read it before their next shift.
- **Safety alert** — Broadcast an urgent safety warning (equipment failure, weather event, security incident) to all locations or affected sites.
- **Policy change** — Share an updated HR policy, code of conduct revision, or compliance requirement and track acknowledgment for your records.
- **Promotional information** — Distribute new campaign assets, pricing updates, or promotional materials to store staff ahead of a launch date.

## How recipients see Heads Up

When a Heads Up is sent, recipients receive:

1. A **push notification** on their mobile device (if the Supa app is installed)
2. An **in-app banner** when they next open Supa — the announcement appears prominently at the top of their screen and requires a tap to dismiss
3. A persistent entry in their **Heads Up** inbox until they acknowledge it

Unacknowledged announcements remain visible in the recipient's inbox and continue to show as unread in your dashboard.

<Warning>
  Heads Up is a one-way broadcast tool. Recipients cannot reply to a Heads Up message. If you need a two-way conversation — for example, to answer questions about a new procedure — use **Team Chat** instead and link it to the relevant topic.
</Warning>
