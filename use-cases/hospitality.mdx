A guest's first impression of a room takes about three seconds. A stain on the duvet, a broken lamp, a toilet that wasn't properly cleaned — any one of these ends up in a review that your next hundred potential guests will read. Managing room readiness and facility maintenance across multiple properties with rotating housekeeping and maintenance teams is genuinely difficult without a system. Supa gives property managers photo proof of every room inspection, a live maintenance task queue, and a single dashboard covering all their properties — so a poor guest experience stops being a surprise and starts being a preventable event.

## The challenge

Guest satisfaction depends on execution that happens out of sight: housekeeping working through a room in 20 minutes, a maintenance technician replacing a light bulb before the next arrival, a pool attendant running safety checks that no manager witnesses. When any of these fail, the guest is the first to notice.

Across multiple properties, the problem multiplies. A property manager responsible for three hotels and two serviced apartment buildings cannot physically inspect every room before every check-in. Without photo evidence, they're managing on the assumption that things went well — and assumptions fail at the worst moments.

## How Supa solves it

Supa replaces assumption with evidence. Housekeeping staff complete room inspection checklists on their phone, photographing the bed, bathroom, amenities, and any items that need attention. Property managers see completed inspections in real time and can review photo evidence for any room in any property without being on-site.

Maintenance requests follow a structured workflow: a staff member reports a broken asset, the task is automatically assigned to the relevant technician, and the resolution requires photo proof of the fix. Property managers can see every open maintenance task across all properties, filtered by urgency and deadline.

<Note>
GPS verification ensures that housekeeping and maintenance staff complete checklists from within the property boundary. Contractors working under SLA agreements cannot submit completion reports from off-site.
</Note>

## How property managers benefit

Property managers and area managers covering multiple properties get a consolidated view that would otherwise require constant travel.

<Steps>
  <Step title="See all properties on a single dashboard">
    Every checklist completion, open maintenance task, and compliance score across all your properties appears in one place. Filter by property, date, or category.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get alerts when tasks are overdue">
    Set deadline thresholds for high-priority tasks — a pre-arrival room inspection that's still incomplete 30 minutes before check-in triggers an automatic alert to the supervisor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review photo evidence without visiting">
    Browse timestamped room inspection photos for any property from your phone or desktop. Approve, reject, or flag rooms for re-inspection without being on-site.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track maintenance resolution end to end">
    From the moment a broken asset is reported to the moment it's repaired and photo-confirmed, you have a complete, searchable record of every maintenance event at every property.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Contractor GPS verification is particularly valuable for SLA compliance. If your cleaning or maintenance contracts specify on-site completion times, Supa's geofenced checklists provide irrefutable confirmation — or flag non-compliance — without requiring your staff to be present.
</Note>
